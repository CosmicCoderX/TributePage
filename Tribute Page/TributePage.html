Nikola Tesla

The visionary inventor who revolutionized electricity and pioneered wireless technology

Here's a timeline of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856: Tesla is born on July 10 in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1875: Tesla enrolls at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz to study electrical engineering, becoming fascinated by electricity.
  • 1881: Tesla moves to Budapest and conceives the idea of a rotating magnetic field, crucial for his AC motor design.
  • 1884: Tesla emigrates to the United States, briefly working for Thomas Edison, where they clash over AC and DC systems.
  • 1887: Tesla opens his lab and develops the AC induction motor, changing the future of electrical power distribution.
  • 1888: Tesla’s AC motor gains support from George Westinghouse, leading to the "War of Currents" against Edison’s DC.
  • 1891: Tesla invents the Tesla Coil, a key device for radio, television, and electronics.
  • 1893: At the Chicago World’s Fair, Tesla and Westinghouse demonstrate the capabilities of AC power.
  • 1895: Tesla's New York lab is destroyed by fire, costing him years of research, yet he continues work on wireless energy.
  • 1899: Tesla conducts high-voltage experiments in Colorado Springs, famously generating artificial lightning.
  • 1901-1905: Tesla works on the Wardenclyffe Tower project, aiming for global wireless communication but abandons it due to financial issues.
  • 1915: Tesla and Edison are rumored for the Nobel Prize in Physics but neither receives it; Tesla struggles financially.
  • 1931: Tesla is celebrated on his 75th birthday by the American Institute of Electrical Engineers.
  • 1943: Tesla dies on January 7 in New York City at age 86, leaving a lasting legacy in AC power and wireless technology.

"Tesla was both a dreamer and a doer, a man who saw the world not as it was, but as it could be, transforming imagination into reality."

-- W. Bernard Carlson
